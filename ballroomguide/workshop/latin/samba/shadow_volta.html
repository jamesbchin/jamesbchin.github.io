Shadow Travelling Volta (Bronze)

This is a Travelling Volta danced in R Shadow Position, Man and Lady using the same foot. It may commence with RF, moving to L, curving up to 3/8 to R over 1-7, or commenced with LF, moving to R, and curving up to 3/8 to L (teaching count "1 a 2 a 3 a 4"). The Travelling Volta may be continued for one or two additional bars of music

Preceding Figures

to Travelling Volta commenced with RF

Bronze Foot Change 1 (Closed Position to R Shadow Position)
Gold Foot Change 8 (RSP to R Shadow Position)

Preceding Figures

When already in R Shadow Position, using same foot as partner

Bronze LF Samba Walk - LF Travelling Bota Fogo Fwd - Shadow Travelling Volta commenced with LF
Silver Shadow Circular Volta Commenced with LF
Gold Cruzados Walks or Locks ended with weight on LF

Following Figures

All on same foot as partner

Bronze LF Samba Walk - LF Travelling Bota Fogo Fwd - Shadow Travelling Volta commenced with LF
Silver Shadow Circular Volta commenced with LF
Gold "Same Foot" Bota Fogo to R Contra Position - Cruzados Walks or Locks commenced with LF

Note

 The last two steps of Shadow Travelling Volta may be replaced with 2 and 3 of a Bota Fogo

Preceding Figures

to Travelling Volta commenced with LF

Silver Foot Change 3 (PP or Open PP to R Shadow Position)

Preceding Figures

When already in R Shadow Position, using same foot as partner

Bronze RF Samba Walk - RF Travelling Bota Fogo Fwd - Shadow Travelling Volta commenced with RF
Silver Shadow Circular Volta commenced with RF
Gold Cruzados Walks or Locks ended with weight on RF

Following Figures

Bronze Foot Change 2 (R Shadow Position to Closed Position)
Silver Foot Change 4 (R Shadow Position to Closed Position)

Following Figures

All on same foot as partner

Bronze RF Samba Walk - RF Travelling Bota Fogo Fwd - Shadow Travelling Volta commenced with RF
Silver Shadow Circular Volta commenced with RF
Gold "Same Foot" Bota Fogo to R Contra Position - Cruzados Walks or Locks commenced with RF

Development

(Silver) After 2 bars of Travelling Volta, continue with 2 bars of Circular Volta or vice versa. Teaching count "1 a 2 a 3 a 4 a 5 a 6 a 7 a 8"
