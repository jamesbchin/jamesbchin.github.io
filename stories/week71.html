Week 71


Like a Wisp of Smoke

"Heyy, is anyone sitting here?"

Jessica looked down at the word counter of her Microsoft Word document. 26 words. 26 more words in this obituary than she had this morning, when she arrived at the cafe. She sighed and closed her laptop, before lifting her cup of tea up to her mouth to take a sip. She looked up, and almost spit her tea out. The man standing in front of her was, quite the specimen. Tall, cleanly shaven, hair with just enough product in it the hold shape but not enough to look tacky. He had a blue blazer on over a navy sweater vest. His hand sat the chair in front of her.

"Yes, my friend is grabbing a drink right now. She'll be right back."

He looked around. The cafe was quiet; only a few people were still there that late into the night. It was near closing time; the staff could be seen cleaning up the kitchen.

"Are you sure? I don't see anyone on line, and the pickup area looks pretty empty too," he said. "I was going to ask if you minded if I joined you but, since you lied about having a friend here I assume you do mind."

Jessica sat there with her face starting to turn red. "I'm sorry, I didn't want to be rude. I just, need to be alone right now."

The cheeky smile on his face turned into a frown. "I'm sorry. I understand. I hope you have a lovely day... I mean night. Or evening."

The strange dude turned to leave. Jessica looked at him suspiciously. It wouldn't be the first time some weird guy hit on her, and she was sure it wouldn't be the last. After a moment, she rolled her eyes, followed by a small smile. She called out, "Hey! Hold up."

He was almost at the door when he looked back at her. "What's up?"

"Why did you come over?"

He just looked at her for a second, before saying, "To be honest, I just kinda needed to talk to someone. You see, my father just passed away and I went on a walk to get some air and well, this cafe is where we used to grab morning tea every morning before..." He stopped mid-sentence. "Anyway, that's enough of that. What are you up to?

***

The cafe closed not too long after. Her word count still read 26 words. The two of them left the cafe, and he offered to walk her home.

It was a chilly evening. It was apparent in the brisk air that winter was coming. He took off his jacket and handed it to her, as they walked through the streets of the city.

Their conversation began with stories of their parents, him his father and her her mother. The exchanged treasured memories, and also regrets. As they continued walking, they began talking about other things, like work and their personal lives. They talked about his dog and her cat. They talked about the terrible rent prices and the struggles of the increasing cost of living. They talked about how they've both always wondered why suspicious smoke is always venting out from the streets of the city. They walked for miles and miles, but eventually they made it to her front door.

Jessica offered the jacket back, and he took it and placed it over his back.

"Thank you for the evening. I'm glad I decided to stop by your table."

She offered him a hug. "Thank you too!"

They let go, slowly releasing each other from their arms. He looked into her eyes, and smiled. "I'll see you around, Jessica."

He turned around, and walked away. She walked up the stairs to her apartment building. As she pulled out her keys, she turned back around. "Wait! We should exchange phone numbers!"

But he was gone, like a wisp of smoke, lifted by the winter air.
