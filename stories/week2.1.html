Week 2.1


"Something bothering you?"

Startled, the kid takes his forehead out of his hands, immediately sitting straight up, as if someone had poked his side and ran away. He took a quick look around the station, trying to figure out who was talking to him.

He sat alone on a wooden bench. The station was quiet, there were only a few people around. Some with their noses in books, other with their dreary eyes glued to their smartphone screens. A few were sleeping, leaning on the poles holding up the station roof. It was late, the kid was coming back from his job working as a barista at the local late night coffee shop.

The voice spoke again, "Stop looking around doofus, I'm on the phone. You must be using your new earbuds again."

"Oh… right… sorry about that."

"It's good dude, no biggie. What's stressing you out?"

He didn't remember calling anyone, much less who he would call at this hour. But he appreciated the company nonetheless.

"What if I really am just a terrible person?"

"What makes you say that?"

He leans back in the chair, thinking back to his earlier interaction with his classmate at school.

"Well, I told, what's his name, Stephen? I told him that he was a terrible programmer with no future because he didn't finish his part of the group project in time."

"Well, is he a terrible programmer?"

"Well, to be honest, I am a much better programmer than he is. I swear Brenden wouldn't be able to program a toaster if his life depended on it."

"Well screw him then!"

"But maybe I was too hard on him, you know? What if he was just busy or somet…"

"No, evidently this guy is useless. You need to drop him."

"You sure?

"Look, is he useless? Does he contribute anything to the team?"

He pauses for a moment. He thinks for a moment, and then stands up.

"You know what, you're right. Who needs Brayden anyways."

"Yeah there you go!"

"He's probably one of those babies that cries to their babysitters."

"That's right!"

A light appears from the nearby tunnel. A train whistle goes off, and the wind from the incoming train blows by.

"You know what, I'll kick him off the team tomorrow. This god damn kid is bringing down the team."

"That's the spirit!"

The train comes to a stop, and the doors open.

"Thanks man, I really needed that."

"Any time bro."

The kid walks through the double doors. They close and the train starts moving. He takes a seat by the window. He reaches into his pocket and pulls out his earbuds, and starts playing some music.
