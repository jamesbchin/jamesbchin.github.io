Week 76


Penpals

There's a gorge, a short bike ride from my house. It's pretty deep, carved and weathered down by millions of years of erosion by the Steven's River. I always found it funny that the river had the same name as me. Maybe my parents named me after the river? Or maybe it was a coincidence.

When I was a kid, I used to bike down the river path every weekend with my parents. There are bridges, built over the river at regular intervals, so people don't have to walk too far to get across. My parents and I would bike down one side of the river, across a bridge, and the bike back on the other side. Every now and then, we would go a little farther, taking the next bridge before making the longer trip home. Eventually I got too old, or maybe my parents got too old, or too busy, but we stopped going together at some point.

So I started going by myself. Every several months, I would go to a further bridge, continuing the tradition. One of those days, I found a small little cliff overhang, tucked behind the trees. It was purely by chance, I just happened to get a flat tire one day and so when I pulled over to the side of the road, I saw it, just beyond the trees separating the path from the several hundred feet drop.

I brought a chair out there, an Adirondack chair. I brought it over in pieces, to not raise any suspicion. Eventually I had my own little spot, overlooking the gorge that runs through the center of my town.

I go there whenever I need to think, whenever I need to be alone. As far as I know, no one has ever been here besides me, and so it's a great place to just, take a step away from life and everything.

But today, in a large glass bottle, I found a note, and a ballpoint pen. I opened the bottle and pulled out the note and it said, Who are you?

Distressed, but also curious, I opened the pen and wrote down, "Sturge, who are you??" underneath the original message. I resealed everything and left the bottle on the chair.
