Week 18


Today is the first day of the annual Autumn Festival. Although a small town, friends and family from all over come back on this day to celebrate the town's biggest event of the year. The first day of the festival is always on the first Friday night of the season. Kids mark their calendars before they go off to college, so that they can come back Friday afternoon to just make it to the first night. There are fun rides and outdoor games, haunted houses and escape rooms. There are snacks and candy and food and drinks. The night is a fun night for everyone, young and old.

"I thought I'd find you up here," a voice said, followed by the very distinct sound of a shuffling pair of converse across concrete. He shuffled over to their favorite spot on the overlook and stood beside her. He places his hand on her shoulder for a second, before lifting both arms around the overlook fence. They stood in silence for a while, listening to the sounds and watching the lights of the festival. The sky was just starting to change color, from the brilliant blue of the day to a slightly... orange-y blue.

She picked her head up, looking away from the festival and towards the small, lightly gray colored clouds up in the sky.

"I got my last college letter today," she said, softly, and monotoned.

"Yeah I... I heard. It's why I figured you'd be up here."

She turned to him, her face stone cold, emotionless. "I came up here to be alone."
