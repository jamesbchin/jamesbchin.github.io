Week 75


A Moment in Time

After years of setting up a massive server, capable of storing several thousands of terrabytes of data, with unbelievable bandwidths, the prototype is complete. A device capable of storing a full virtual model of the planet, a single frozen moment in time. And all it took was a gigantic computer, and a virus.

Several months ago, a virus was created and spread out amongst the constellation of devices that make up the Earth. Over time, it hid, propogating in the shadows. Eventually every single connected device, every computer and phone and refrigerator and satelite had a copy of the virus. And they lay dormant, sleeping, until the master server was built, and called the start function. Once called, every device uses its full suite of internal and external sensors, and creates as good of a 3D map of its surroundings as it can, and sends it to the central server.

Once the data is collected, the server cautiously stitches every mini environment together, until it completes its single purpose in life: to save a single moment in time, forever.
