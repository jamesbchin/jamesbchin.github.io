one story per week

Week 1

Prompt: "The mailman" (Natalia Romano Spica)


"No matter if it rains or snows, the mail must go through!"

A smartphone's custom alarm rings on a bedside table in a small town in the middle of somewhere. A pair of arms emerge, stretching out under the bed covers. A loud yawn, and the arms come down. 6:00 AM; the start of a mailman's day. He jumps out of bed, ready to start his daily routine. Takes a shower, brushes his teeth, and then onto the kitchen where he cooks his breakfast: oatmeal with various fruits (raspberry is his favorite), some milk, and a touch of honey. Before eating, he opens the window to let in the cool autumn air. He takes a deep breath, and smiles. A perfect day to go for a walk.

The porcelain bowl, stainless steel pot and spoon are rinsed in the sink, and then placed into the washing machine. Dish soap, close the door, button two, five, and then the one at the very end. One last quick trip to the restroom, and then it's time to put on some proper clothes.

A dull blue bag, worn but obviously well maintained, hangs from a hook mounted to the wall next to the door. The color of the bronze hook looks polished where the bag strap sits. The bag has a pocket for a water bottle, some extra socks and a little pouch full of daily essentials: pen, paper, house keys, etc. A blue jacket and hat both rest on a simple coat hanger, also by the door. The post office logo sits proudly on the jacket shoulder sleeve.

The mailman sits down on a little stool by the door and he puts on his freshly shined shoes. Left foot, then right foot, "Double Slip Knot" on both. He stands, puts on his jacket and hat, and throws on his bag over his shoulder. He opens the door, walks through, and takes out his keys.

The fire alarm rings.
