Week 72


The Balcony

The Chris Jean Convention Center used to be a major landmark in the small city (or large town) of Port Johnson. It was a large glass building, the size of several stadiums. It wasn't particularly tall for it's width, but it was pretty wide; the building stood taller than most in the general downtown area.

At the present day though, it lays dormant. Due to bankruptsies and company mergers and other large tech giant business crap, the largest company in the area, and therefore all of its employees, were moving to a "real city". Port Johnson is being abandoned.

Peter Johnson walked up the stairs. The "Canal Staircase" is what the local high school band unofficially named Staircase C, it being the closest to the canal and all. The Canal Staircase was where all the suspicious high school band schenanigans used to happen, because it was almost always abandoned.

Before performances, Peter used to come early and walk up the staircase up to the top floor. There was a balcony up there, and one of the doors had a broken lock.

Seventh floor, eighth floor, ninth floor... three more floors to go. Peter pulled his phone out of his backpack, and checked his notifications for the first time since the previous morning. Zero new messages. He let out a resigned sigh, and put his phone back into his bag.

He continued to walk up, not noticing the footsteps behind him, walking in step with him, a couple floors below.

The man below wore a long black jacket and black sunglasses. Underneath was even more black; he wore a black turtleneck long sleave shirt and loose fitting black slacks. He walked up the stairs lightly, making very little sound.

Peter got to the top floor, and walked through his normal door. The rapid shift from dark to light was blinding at first but, once his eyes adjusted, the view was as astonishing as ever.

He walked over to the edge of the balcony and dropped his backpack at the base of the railing. He knelt down and opened the bag, pulling out a couple of unsealed envelopes, addressed to various people. Each envelope contained a handwritten letter. Peter opened each one and made sure the name on the letter matched the name and address on the envelope, before sealing each of them, one by one.

After sealing the last letter, he stood up, and threw the letters over the side of the building. They each fluttered away, each in a separated direction. He took a breath and climbed up to the top of the railing. He counted down from ten.

The door behind him opened, slowly and carefully, and the man in the jacket stepped out. He waved his hand and muttered something under his breath as Peter got to "two... one..."

Peter closed his eyes and took one last breath before stepping out. He fell for a second before... an energy field appeared below him, catching his fall as if it were a net. It lifted him back up to the balcony.

The man with the long jacket closed the door and smiled, as he walked back down the stairs.
