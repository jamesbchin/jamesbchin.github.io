2015-1-8. A Google Map from Taicheng, Guangdong, China to Dong Lian Cun. Taishan Shi, Jiangmen Shi, Guangdong. (Note: Baidu map has a more direct route to Huang Village. If anyone is interested in the Baidu map, please email me. Thanks.)

Google Maps does not show roads to Huang Village (横村). The closest village is Dong Lian Cun (东联村).
From Dong Lian Cun to Huang Village is less than one mile. You are almost there.

Specific instructions to reach the fork, which points to the 5 hamlets of Huang Village:

  1. Click on "More options" in the upper left corner.
  2. Click on the "+" sign in the lower right corner 4 times.
  3. Click on the "Earth" option in the lower left corner.
  4. Drag your map so that the end point (Dong Lian Cun) in on the bottom center. This is Sanhe Market (三合圩).
    The scale in the lower right corner should have a scale showing 500 ft (which is about 5/8" on the screen).
  5. About 400 ft NW of the end point you take the road going north, crossing the Sanhe bridge (三合桥).
  6. Make a right turn after crossing the bridge.
  7. Another 3,000 ft you come to another bridge (Guan Bu Bridge 官步桥). Do not cross this bridge.
  8. On your left is the Huang Village Arch. You made it! Congrats! You are now in Huang Village.
  9. To get to the hamlets, go throught the arch and keep going until you come across a fork.
    This fork has signs pointing the 5 hamlets of Huang Village. Good Luck!

General instructions on using the map:

  • Click on the "+" or "-" signs in the bottom right corner to see more or less details, respectively.
  • Click on "More options" in the upper left corner to explore more options., such as driving instructions.
  • When you click on "More options" you get a new tab or window, depending on your browser setup.
    To get back to the original map., just close this tab or window.

2010-05-16. Chén Coat of Arms

此圖爲陳姓圖騰. 圖騰是古代氏族的族徽. 陳姓是以封地爲姓氏的. 陳本來是太昊氏炎帝及其後裔的天文中心. 陳姓圖騰由 [ 東 ] 和 [ 太陽升降的階梯 ] 組成:東爲日在木中, 木又叫扶木或建木 ( 扶木即扶桑,它和建木一樣都是古代神話傳説中的一種樹木, 用來觀測太陽的高度, 以此來制定曆法 ) 等. 樹立 [ 扶桑 ( 古代神話傳説中的一種樹, 用來觀測太陽的高度, 以此來制定曆法 ) ] 或 [ 扶木 ] 等的地方爲 [ 陳 ], 古代有陳倉, 陳留, 陳地等, 它們都是天文觀測中心, 也都是當時的首都或京城.

This image is the coat of arms of the Chen Family. The coat of arms was the Old Clan's emblem. The surname Chen came from the enfeoffed land called Chen. Chen was originally the astronomical center of the descendants of Taihao and Yandi. The Chen coat of arms has the form of "east" and "sunset and sunrise step ladder" and later became "sun in the middle of tree". This "tree" was also called the "help tree" or "construct tree". "Help tree" was also called "help mulberry". The "help tree" or "construct tree" was an ancient mythical tree used to observe the altitude of the sun to create the model for a calendar. In ancient times there were place names beginning with Chen, such as Chen Cang, Chen Liu, and others. These places were all astronomical observatory centers and later became capital cities.

Translated by Gene M Chin (posted on 5/16/2010)

 

2010-05-16. Generation Poem

班次聯 bāncìlián or 派字歌 pàizìgē is a poem of various lengths. Ours is four lines with five characters each. It began in the thirteenth generation from Chén Mó 陳謨 (1173 - 1242 ) with a four-line poem written by Chén Bái Shā 陳白沙 (1428 - 1500), a Ming dynasty naturalist thinker, life scientist, poet, calligrapher, and educator. It was continued with another twenty characters written by Chén Bó Táo 陳伯陶 (1855 -1930), a Qing dynasty writer, until the 52nd generation. A person's hào 號 comes from the generation poem for that person's particular generation. For example, my grandfather belonged to the 24th generation and his hào 號 was Xù Yè 煦業. One of the characters for his hào 號 must contain Yè 業 and the other character was chosen by his father. The hào 號 was conferred in a ceremony at our ancestrial temple when he was married. This custom was not universally practiced at all villages. Some males and females may also be given a hào 號 even at a very young age. The hào 號 is a title of respect and from the time the title was conferred my grandfather was known as Chén Xù Yè 陳煦業 from that point forward. The hào 號 also defines a person's bānpài 班派, that is, a person's generation. This is especially important when one addresses one's elders. Thus, if a person's hào 號 has the character Yè 業 in it, I know immediately that I should address him as granduncle, Gōng 公, unless he was my grandfather, in which case, I would address him as Yé 爺, paternal grandfather. Males in our village who were my father's generation (25th), Guāng 光, would be my uncles. If they were older than my father, they would be addressed as Bó 伯. If they were younger than my father, they would be addressed as Shū 叔. If males in our village are in the same generation as me and older than I am, I would address them Gē 哥. If they were younger, I address them by their given name.

新會陳白沙提文起祖傳十三世始立
聖文萬世昌。應朝瑞國祥。德業光前遠。遵法永延長。

東莞陳伯陶提文起祖傳三十三世始立
仁厚承恩澤。書香啟俊良。邦家常有耀。續學自名揚。

The first 20 characters were written by Chen Bai Sha (1428-1500 CE) for Chen Mo’s fourth son Chen Wen Qi starting from the 13th generation.

Eminent classical literature will prosper for many generations In response to an auspicious dynasty and the kindness of a nation. The honor of moral conduct and achievement will shine into the distant future. Obeying the law will be protracted forever.

The second 20 characters were written by Chen Bo Tao (1854-1930 CE) as a continuation of Chen Bai Sha’s poem for the next 20 generations until the 52nd generation.

Benevolence and generosity will be graciously extended to the people. A literary atmosphere will enlighten the talented and conscionable people. The glory of one's fatherland will be dazzling. Continuous study will develop one's fame.

2009-12-23. A new version of the Huang Village Family Tree has been uploaded today. It now traces our ancestry back to the Yellow Emperor (Huangdi) who reigned from 2697 BCE to 2598 BCE. Portraits of Huangdi, Shundi, and the five Chen Dynasty emperors have been added. I am now working on getting portraits of Chen Hugong (the first Chen) and Chen Shigong.

Thank you, Steve Chan, for getting more photos for the site and adding more members of your father's family. It was a pleasure meeting you and your daughter, Jessica.

The numbers in the first column of each chart are generation numbers. The first number counts from Huangdi (The Yellow Emperor). The second number counts from Mangong (Chen Hugong), the progenitor of the Chen/Chin/Chan/Chinn/Chun/Tan/Tran clan. The third number counts from Shigong, the protector of Yingchuanshijun 穎川始郡, which is where most of the southern Chen/Chin/Chan/Chinn/Chun/Tan/Tran people came from. The fourth number is counting Chen Mo, the first of seven sons of Chen Hui. Many of the Chens in the Taishan District in Guangdong are descended from Chen Hui.

Posted by Gene M Chin (12/23/09)
